Introduction

Taipei Tech campus is located at the apex of the Taipei Metro system. Not only can students enjoy a world-class education at Taipei Tech, but they also gain the opportunity to enjoy the rich traditional culture that Taipei offers. Attractions such as Taipei 101, the Palace Museum, and the Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall are in close proximity to Taipei Tech.

History & Philosophy

Established in 1912, Taipei Tech is among one of the oldest universities in Taiwan. It was first established during the Japanese Colonial Period as the Auxiliary Industrial Institute of the Education Affairs Office of the Civil Administration Department. The Institute consisted of only three departments (Woodcraft, Metalwork and Electrical Engineering) ushered in an era of industrial education in Taiwan.

Social Responsibility

Taipei Tech is committed to the participation in social responsibility and the promotion of sustainable development. University social responsibility and the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) have been incorporated into the key initiatives of Taipei Tech’s medium- and long-term plan. The University Social Responsibility (USR) Office at Taipei Tech integrates the strength and expertise of all departments and connect the expertise to social innovation and sustainability to further enhance the development and prosperity of the local community. The USR Office also serves as an educational training base of USR education which encourages innovative teaching and social participation. Taipei Tech also launches the Sustainability Report and SDGs Special Issue annually with the contents of school projects that target at achieving the sustainable development goals. Our university will continue to enahnce teachers’ and students' engagement in global sustainability, hoping to achieve greater and positive influence on our community and the world.
